# Hi! My name is Cam.
I'm a data and investigative journalist based out of Chicago, and you can usually find me trekking around the Midwest for business and pleasure.

📰 **Right now**, I'm a **data reporter** at **Chicago Public Media**, which publishes the **Chicago Sun-Times** and **WBEZ**, Chicago's NPR station. I cover everything under the sun that impacts people in and around Chicago, on air, on the web and in print. Some recent stories of mine include looking at the [record number of 311 calls related to severe weather in Chicago](https://chicago.suntimes.com/weather/2026/06/16/chicago-severe-weather-complaints-311-calls-record-breaking-amount-downed-trees) and the [increase of short-term rentals in the neighborhoods around the Obama Presidential Center](https://www.wbez.org/housing/2026/06/05/barack-obama-airbnb-presidential-center-chicago-affordable-housing). If you have ideas for a story, or something you want me to take a look at, you can reach me at [crodriguez@wbez.org](mailto:crodriguez@wbez.org). 💌 

🔎 **Before that**, I was a Local Investigations Fellow at the New York Times, covering [the spread of warehouses across Illinois](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/05/12/us/warehouse-hubs-semi-trucks-chicago.html), and was part of a team of reporters across the country digging into issues impacting their local markets, from their local markets. (In this case, it meant I was working across the state of Illinois!)

📜 **In the past**, I've worked with teams at [Chalkbeat](https://github.com/chalkbeat), [USATODAY](https://github.com/usatoday), WTTW/Chicago PBS, [MuckRock](https://github.com/muckrock), [South Side Weekly](https://github.com/southsideweekly), [Illinois Answers/The Better Government Association](https://github.com/bettergov), Voice of San Diego, the Detroit Free Press, and others. I also was the managing editor at 14 East Magazine, the award-winning student magazine at DePaul University, where I studied journalism, geography and community-focused reporting. You might also see me around as a mentor for different programs (like the [Dow Jones News Fund](https://dowjonesnewsfund.org/)) or teaching, speaking and coaching at conferences, like those run by [Investigative Reporters and Editors](https://www.ire.org/).

🖥️ **On here**, you can usually find me tooling around with spatial data, tinkering with random projects, or hitting dead ends. (Hey, everyone needs a sandbox!) Some of my favorite projects here are my graduate GIS project on [Mexican tourism industry expansion](https://github.com/cam-rodriguez/mx_coastal), [digitized and georeferenced Sanborn map data](https://github.com/cam-rodriguez/sanborn), and a [1950s demographic map brought to life in the present](https://github.com/cam-rodriguez/settlement-map).
